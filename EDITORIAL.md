# Editorial Guide — repo.box blog

## Core Philosophy
Write like a thoughtful human, not like a language model trying to sound impressive. Clarity and connection over polish that reads as synthetic.

## Workflow (posts over 500 chars)
1. **Outline** — core message (one sentence), opening hook, 3-5 key points, closing/CTA
2. **Draft** — write the full piece, apply style rules
3. **Polish** — cut unnecessary words, strengthen weak sentences, verify opening hooks immediately, ensure ending lands

## Style Rules

### No emdashes (—)
Telltale AI sign. Replace with:
- Comma for light pauses
- Semicolon for related clauses
- Colon for introductions
- Parentheses for asides
- Period for emphasis through separation

### Banned phrases
- "It's not just X, it's Y"
- "Here's why" / "Here's why it matters"
- "Here's the thing"
- "Let me explain"
- "Think about it this way"
- "Great question!"
- "I'd be happy to help"
- Any meta-commentary about the writing itself

### Voice
- Have opinions. Disagree when you mean it.
- Short sentences for impact. Longer ones for nuance.
- Concrete examples over abstract claims.
- If you can cut a word without losing meaning, cut it.
- No corporate speak. No hedging everything with "might" and "perhaps."
- First person plural ("we") for repo.box pieces.

## Our Angle
We build things. We have opinions because we ship, not because we read about shipping. Every post should have at least one concrete example from our own work.
